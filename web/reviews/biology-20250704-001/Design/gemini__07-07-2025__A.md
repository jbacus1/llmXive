## Specific Recommendations for Improvement

To advance to implementation planning, this document must be revised to include the following:

### 1. Refine Project Scope
The project should either be re-scoped to focus on 1-2 closely related cell types (e.g., different neuronal subtypes) or be explicitly positioned as a longer, 5-year program with a detailed pilot phase in Year 1.

### 2. Provide Experimental Specificity
The revised document must specify:
- **Species**: Human or mouse?
- **Source**: Primary tissues, cell lines, or iPSCs? Provide justification for the choice
- **Specific Cell Subtypes**: E.g., "CD4+ T-cells," "cortical neurons," "cardiomyocytes"

### 3. Detail the Computational Plan
The "deep learning" section should be expanded to define the model's input data, architecture (e.g., CNN, transformer), and the precise prediction task (e.g., "predict gene expression changes in response to transcription factor perturbation").

### 4. Justify Whole-Genome Bisulfite Sequencing (WGBS)
WGBS is expensive and data-intensive. The document should justify why this is necessary over more targeted methylation assays (e.g., EPIC arrays), especially within the proposed budget.

### 5. Develop a Concrete Risk Mitigation Plan
For each identified risk, provide a specific, quantitative QC check and a clear plan of action if that check fails. For example: "If scRNA-seq yields <70% viable cells for a given sample, that sample will be discarded and the isolation protocol will be re-optimized."

### 6. Re-evaluate the Timeline
The timeline must be adjusted to reflect the refined scope. Include milestones for each phase, such as "Completion of pilot data generation for two cell types by Month 9."
