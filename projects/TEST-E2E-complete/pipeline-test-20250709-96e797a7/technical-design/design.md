Title: Exploring the Potential of DialoGPT-medium Model for Mathematical Theorem Proposal Generation

1. Abstract
This research proposal supports the exploration of the DialoGPT-medium Model's potential to generate preliminary ideas for mathematical theorem discovery. Not primarily focused on proving new mathematical theorems, the project instead intends to investigate artificial intelligence's role in initial hypothesis formulation in mathematical research.

2. Introduction and Background
The compelling effectiveness of mathematics and Artificial Intelligence(AI) combination is increasingly recognized. However, the role of AI in the proposition of new mathematical theorem ideas remains less explored. This research intends to assess if the DialoGPT-medium, a proficient AI model, can be effective in this context.

3. Research Objectives and Hypotheses
The project seeks to determine if DialoGPT-medium, trained with a dataset of existing mathematical theorems, can propose a substantial number of preliminary theorem ideas that meet a specific standard of originality and consistency, as evaluated by a panel of mathematical experts.

4. Methodology and Approach
For this research, an experimental and analytical approach will engage the Docker and Jupyter interfaces. A collection of mathematical theorems from open-source databases, such as Arxiv, EuDML, and zbMath, will serve as the model's training data. The model will be trained using TensorFlow and PyTorch libraries to ensure optimal results. The proposed theorems' novelty and feasibility will be determined by applying a qualified panel of at least five experts in theoretical mathematics. 

5. Technical Implementation Details
The implementation outline entails using the Python programming language, libraries such as TensorFlow and Pytorch and DialoGPT-medium model for training purposes. The code will be written within the Docker and Jupyter settings to ensure standardization, reproducibility, and adequate security.

6. Expected Outcomes and Timeline
For a one-year span, the project will focus on four phases: data collection and cleaning (2 months), model training (4 months), theorem proposition (4 months), and evaluation of these propositions (2 months). Successful proposal of novel mathematical theorem ideas by the model is the anticipated outcome.

7. Resource Requirements
Resources needed include the DialoGPT-medium model, GPU for model training, mathematical theorem data, Python language, and data analysis software. An interdisciplinary team, consisting of mathematicians, statisticians, and machine-learning researchers, is also required. The project demands research literature on AI in Mathematics and its potential applications as well.

8. Risk Assessment
Possible risks my include algorithm underperformance, data bias, and resistance from traditional mathematic research circles. The mitigation plans include in-depth testing of the model, careful data curation to check for bias, and continual dialogue with the mathematical community about the potential benefits of AI-assisted theorem proposition.
