## 4. Climate-Smart Agriculture Literature

### 4.1 Foundational Publications

| Citation | Key Contribution |
|----------|------------------|
> FAO. (2013). *Climate-Smart Agriculture Sourcebook*. Rome: Food and Agriculture Organization. ISBN 978-92-5-107669-5 | **Definition and Framework**: Established the three-pillar CSA approach (productivity, adaptation, mitigation)

| Citation | Key Contribution |
|----------|------------------|
> Lobell, D. B., et al. (2011). Climate Trends and Global Crop Production Since 1980. *Science*, 333(6042), 616-620. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1204531 | **Impact Quantification**: Demonstrated measurable climate impacts on global crop yields

| Citation | Key Contribution |
|----------|------------------|
> IPCC. (2022). *Climate Change 2022: Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability*. Contribution of Working Group II to the Sixth Assessment Report. Cambridge University Press. | **Risk Assessment**: Comprehensive assessment of climate risks to food systems

### 4.2 Methodology References

| Citation | Application in Project |
|----------|------------------------|

| Citation | Application in Project |
|----------|------------------------|
> Thornton, P. K., et al. (2014). Mapping climate vulnerability and poverty in Africa to assist resource allocation. *Global Environmental Change*, 28, 151-162. | **Vulnerability Indexing**: Methodology for combining climate and socioeconomic data

| Citation | Application in Project |
|----------|------------------------|
> Vermeulen, S. J., Campbell, B. M., & Ingram, J. S. I. (2012). Climate Change and Food Systems. *Annual Review of Environment and Resources*, 37, 195-222. | **Food Systems Approach**: Integrated analysis framework
