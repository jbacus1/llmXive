Title: Enhancing Qubit Coherence using Bi2Se3 Topological Insulators in Superconducting Quantum Circuits

1. Abstract
This proposal delves into the application possibility of Bismuth Selenide (Bi2Se3), a recognized 3D topological insulator, in advancing the qubit coherence time in superconducting quantum circuits. The research question investigates whether Bi2Se3 can escalate qubit coherence times by at least 10% leveraging its topologically protected surface states that potentially reduce noise interferences. Methods include synthesizing high-quality Bi2Se3 thin films, developing hybrid Bi2Se3-superconductor structures, implementing Transmon-like qubits and performing quantum control experiments using pulse-shaping microwave control techniques. Using the insight gained about the practical deployment of topological insulators in quantum computing, the research anticipates advancing quantum computing efficiency.

2. Introduction and Background
Topological insulators, as typified by Bi2Se3, possess aberrant attributes that allow the fabrication of surface-bound qubits with minimized contact with the environment reducing noise interferences. The recent discovery of these insulators with their conductive surfaces presents application prospects in quantum circuits to increase coherence time by reducing susceptibility to decoherence from ambient electric and magnetic fields.

3. Research Objectives and Hypotheses
The project aims to ascertain whether use of Bi2Se3 topological insulator can bolster the qubit coherence time by 10% in superconducting quantum circuits of aluminum. The underpinning hypothesis is based on the unique topological protected surface states of Bi2Se3 creating a fortified environment for qubits, thereby lessening decoherence rates while maintaining low environment-qubit interaction.

4. Methodology and Approach
This research includes synthesizing Bi2Se3 thin films using Molecular Beam Epitaxy, developing hybrid structures of Bi2Se3 and aluminum superconductors, and implementing Transmon-like qubits in such circuits. Quantum control experiments employing pulse-shaping microwave techniques and coherence time measurements using Ramsey Fringe and Spin-Echo techniques will be performed.

5. Technical Implementation Details 
High-quality Bi2Se3 thin films will be synthesized using Molecular Beam Epitaxy and coupled with aluminum superconductor to devise Qubit structures using existing topological superconducting methodologies. Qubit state manipulations and subsequent measurements of state decoherence will be done using pulse-shaping microwave control techniques. Measurement of coherence times will be determined using the Ramsey Fringe and Spin-Echo techniques. ARPES and STM will be used for Bi2Se3 film quality verification, while SQUID microscopy will be used to ascertain the hybrid structure integrity. A control experiment with an all-aluminum device will also be performed.

6. Expected Outcomes and Timeline
A coherence time increase resulting from enhanced shielding is expected after Bi2Se3 implementation in the quantum circuits. A research timeline of 24-36 months is expected, with the milestones of: material synthesis (0-6 months), device fabrication and control experiments (6-12 months), characterization (12-18 months), and carrying out the Bi2Se3 implemented experiments and data analysis (18-36 months).

7. Resource Requirements
Resources needed include a clean room laboratory for fabrication, molecular beam epitaxy system, high-frequency microwave equipment, liquid Helium cryostat for low temperature experiments, and manpower resource for both operation and analysis.

8. Risk Assessment
Potential risks include challenges in achieving high-quality thin film synthesis, difficulty in creating optimal coupling between the states, and managing possible material defects. Strategies for managing these risks include optimization of growth conditions for higher quality films, employing alternative coupling schemes, and improving fabrication techniques respectively.
